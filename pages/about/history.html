For over 160 years the YWCA has been at the forefront of the most critical social movements. From women’s empowerment and civil rights, to affordable housing and pay equity, to violence prevention and health care; every YWCA chapter has continuosly supported women, families and their communities. In the city of San Pedro, the Harbor Area, and South Bay the YWCA has made it a mission to create a positive impact of progress and change. Hear our story.

1917-1921

Foundations from the San Pedro War Work Council

In November of 1917, six women came together to form the San Pedro War Work Council. The Councils were part of a program sponsored by the National YWCA to address unique needs of women during World War I; including access to housing, recreation,and meeting places around training camps where women could visit friends and relatives in the service.

1918

The San Pedro Hospitality Center

Julia Morgan received recommendations that called for “a club and recreation center to compete successfully with public dances, motion pictures shows, and streets filled with men in uniform; housing with facilities for laundry and cooking breakfast; and rooms for 20 girls [to] provide for the acute emergency.”

1921

Indpendence and Member Affiliation

Members petitioned for the San Pedro Hospitality Center to become an independent YWCA. This special request was granted, since the YWCA had a policy of having only one independent association per jurisdiction (in this case, Los Angeles). Julia Morgan would return occasionally to make additions to the new YWCA of San Pedro.

1940-1954

Hospitality to Community Help Center

As the facility changed over the years, the YWCA made a number of modifications to accomadate the needs of the community. Redesigned for needy women and children the facilities included six small rooms for girls, a communal sleeping porch, laundry, and bathing rooms.
